Securing what matters most.

That's why SafeTShield was created -- to identify harmful content and lead our children in the right direction.

~ How it works ~

Wait for a new site.

Like a well-trained dog, SafeTShield sits in the metaphorical corner of your browser, watching and waiting patiently for a child to open a new tab, and then website. Once the child does this, an Event Listener is triggered that begins the process of actually going through the site with a fine-tooth comb to determine the level of potential harm incurred by it.

Parse through the source code.

After the new page has loaded, within a matter of fractions of seconds, SafeTShield converts the page's source code to a string of HTML text, and finally into an array of strings containing sections of text. After creating this array, it is passed through a machine learning model that determines if a section or sections of text contain problematic language, keeping track of the number and severity of the occurrences.

To redirect, or not to redirect?

That is the question. Depending on a variety of factors -- including the length of the innapropriate portion, the number of problematic words or phrases, and the percentage confidence level of the word's designation as harmful, SafeTShield makes a decision on whether to close the website or leave it as is.
